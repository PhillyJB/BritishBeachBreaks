Brighton Beach
East Sussex - Shingle, surfing, Pier...

Brighton is one of Britain's most popular coastal towns and sits on the south coast in Sussex. Backing onto the South Downs its long sand and shingle beaches face into the English Channel. Brighton sits in the centre of a shallow bay which stretches from the headlands at Selsey Bill to Beachy Head.

Camber Sands
East Sussex - Sand & Shingle, Lifeguard service, kite surfing...

Camber Sands is a little unusual for a Sussex beach and the reason is in the name. Yes, sands! Unlike most beaches along the Sussex coast which are lined with pebbles and shingle held in place by a series of groynes, Camber Sands is covered in fine, golden sand. Not only that but Camber is home to the only sand dunes in East Sussex.

Type of beach:

Sand & shingle

Lifeguard service:

Yes

Dog friendly?:

Dogs are allowed between May and September but must be on a lead and restricted between Zones F & H on the beach.

Activities:

Donkey rides
Kite surfing
Swimming/bathing
Wind surfing

Facilities:

Slipway

Postcode:

TN31 7RT

Lepe Beach
Hampshire - Sand & Shingle, Kayaking, wind surfing...

A stony beach that forms part of Lepe Country Park, situated in an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty. Views across to the Isle of Wight and excellent coastal walks featuring pine-fringed cliffs, D-day remains and wild flower meadows.

Sandbanks Beach
Dorset - Sandy, good fishing, putting..

Sandbanks is a spit of golden sand that crosses the mouth of Poole harbour and continues all the way to Bournemouth, a distance of around five kilometers. Dubbed Britain’s Palm Beach, because of astronomical land prices, Sandbanks is well-served by upmarket hotels and restaurants.

Sandy

Lifeguard service:

Yes

Dog friendly?:

dogs are restricted to the area running from the extreme western end of Sandbanks Promenade to Haven Point.

Sennen Cove
Cornwall - Sandy, lifeguard service...

Sennen Cove is the nearest village to Land’s End. An excellent sandy beach extends for a mile, becoming wilder and more dangerous at the northern end. Very popular with surfers and families. Facilities include a pub, cafes, a surf shop, two surf schools and a large car park. Big enough to never feel too overcrowded.

Type of beach:

Sandy

Lifeguard service:

Yes

Dog friendly?:

Dogs are banned during the summer months.

Activities:

Swimming/bathing

Shoeburyness Beach
Essex - Sand & shingle, Jet-skiing, Wind surfing...

The sand and shingle beach of Shoeburyness is backed by a promenade and a large grassy area which is home to any number of beach huts. For those not lucky enough to have the use of one of these huts there is always Uncle Toms Cabin with its light refreshments, ice cream and all the buckets and spades you could wish for.

Sand & shingle

Lifeguard service:

No

Dog friendly?:

Dog restrictions apply and there is a dog exercise area.
